# Portfolio V2.2 research verification

Verification date: 2026-07-14. The Atlas is a relevance-curated implementation map, not a ranking.

## Method

- Canonical primary sources were checked for title, author/organization, year, venue or specification version, and status.
- Recent harness papers remain labeled preprint unless the primary record establishes a conference status.
- FHIR R4 and CDS Hooks v2.0.1 are technical specifications, not peer-reviewed research.
- Relationship edges mean `informs`, `implemented in`, `evaluated by`, `constrains`, `extends`, or `relates to`; they never imply a verified citation unless stated.
- No paper figure, PDF page, long abstract, or publisher logo is reproduced.

## Verified primary records used in the public Atlas

The implementation includes primary links for XGBoost, SHAP, calibration, RAG, Lost in the Middle, context engineering, Ragas, Self-RAG, ReAct, Toolformer, Reflexion, AgentBench, tau-bench, SWE-bench, SWE-agent, representation engineering/activation steering, FHIR R4, and CDS Hooks. Current 2026 harness records are presented as frontier preprints or conference-related preprints according to their primary pages.

## Fetch limitations

Canonical URLs were retained where direct automated retrieval was unavailable because of publisher 403 responses, bot protection, or an unsafe redirect. Those records are not described as freshly full-text inspected. This affected the DOI landing pages for LIME and selective classification, the BMJ page, the PROBAST page, one OpenReview record, and PubMed/PMC browser pages.

## Meta Harness disambiguation

Shailesh's `smgpulse007/meta-harness` open-source project is independent of the 2026 paper titled *Meta-Harness: End-to-End Optimization of Model Harnesses* unless repository history establishes otherwise. The Atlas treats them as separate entities and does not imply authorship, affiliation, derivation, shared code, or endorsement.

## Authored-publication handling

No item is labeled `authored publication` in the public Atlas without a primary author record that was independently verified. Career narrative may mention biomedical research foundations without converting unverified résumé entries into publication claims.

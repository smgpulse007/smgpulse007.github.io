# Portfolio V2.1 visual direction

## Direction

**Operational Editorial — Evidence in Motion**

The interface pairs editorial pacing with one recurring decision thread: input becomes interpretation, a control changes state, a person receives the reason and source, and the trace closes. The thread explains causality rather than decorating the page.

## What remains

- near-black and paper surfaces;
- a restrained jade signal color;
- serif/sans tension;
- compact operational detail;
- static Astro rendering and strong keyboard behavior.

## What changes

- The hero becomes a packet-to-decision story instead of a console.
- The homepage is paced as approximately eight distinct chapters.
- Natural language replaces internal evidence and governance labels in normal reading mode.
- Paper sections create contrast and reduce the single-surface effect.
- Mobile uses a vertical decision story rather than a compressed desktop instrument.

## Flagship visual grammar

| Story | Visual metaphor | Causal idea |
|---|---|---|
| Claims intelligence | packet anatomy and decision trace | conflicting dates route to review with page evidence |
| On-prem document intelligence | privacy boundary and evidence flow | documents stay local while citations cross into the review surface |
| Healthcare analytics platform | Health Index composite and program adoption map | models become a repeatable product used across nine programs |
| LLM Steering | activation hook plus real output comparison | one controlled intervention produces an inspectable, non-generalized output shift |

## Motion and interaction

- Hero autoplay occurs at most once and only when reduced motion is not requested.
- Pause, play, previous, next, and scenario controls remain keyboard operable.
- The complete default sequence exists in server-rendered HTML.
- No random output, fake live data, continuous WebGL, or decorative parallax is used.

## Restraint rules

- one dominant idea per viewport;
- no universal terminal-card template;
- no custom cursor;
- no glassmorphism wall;
- no badge wall;
- no provenance footnotes competing with the primary story;
- no media without a source, commit, license, caption, alt, and public-safety decision.
